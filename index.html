A read on where AI fits in SSINDEX's methodology, and where it does not.

SSINDEX measures what stakeholders actually experience, across more than 60 ESG variables, cross-analyzed between employees, clients, suppliers, communities, and investors. The methodology was built at Yale. The benchmark now holds more than 200,000 evaluations from 40+ countries. In 2024 the platform expanded into Mexico, Colombia, and Peru on top of a Chilean base of 70+ organizations. That product surface is larger than a 19-person team should reasonably carry, and the next wave of competitors is using AI to compress the parts of it that feel most mechanical.

The meta line

The moat is a Yale-designed methodology. The right place for AI in the stack is the ten percent of work that happens before the methodology runs, not the methodology itself.

The 60/30/10 view of the SSINDEX stack

Most of the work is not AI. The part that is, is exactly where the competitive pressure sits.

60%
Traditional software and data engineering
  • Survey infrastructure
  • Multilingual distribution
  • Response storage
  • Benchmark comparison
  • Report generation
Postgres-and-code territory. Reliable, auditable, cheaper than an LLM doing the same job.
30%
Rule-based logic, the methodology itself
  • Scoring rubrics
  • Materiality map
  • Variable weightings inside the Yale methodology
These are the rules that make SSINDEX defensible and should not be handed to a large language model.
10%
Where AI earns its place
  • Response coding across languages
  • Flagging stakeholder responses that contradict stated policy
  • Outlier detection before a human analyst sees the file
  • First-pass drafting of a client-ready report
The ten percent the AI-native competitors are coming from, and where SSINDEX can take the ground back with a scoped orchestration layer that wraps the methodology.
